Statistical Modeling & Time Series

US Real Wages Analysis

Inflation-adjusted wage trend analysis with SARIMA, LSTM, and ensemble forecasting methods, featuring rigorous model validation and bootstrap confidence intervals.

GitHub Repository

Problem Statement

Understanding real wage dynamics is crucial for economic policy, labor market analysis, and individual financial planning. Nominal wage growth often masks the true purchasing power changes experienced by workers, especially during periods of high inflation.

This project develops a comprehensive forecasting framework for US real wages that combines classical econometric methods with modern deep learning approaches, providing both point forecasts and principled uncertainty estimates.

Why This Problem Matters

Real wage forecasting has direct implications for:

  • Monetary policy decisions and inflation targeting
  • Labor market health assessment
  • Retirement planning and long-term financial projections
  • Wage negotiation benchmarks for employers and unions

Most existing analyses focus on nominal wages or simple inflation adjustments. This project provides a more rigorous treatment with proper uncertainty quantification — essential for any serious policy or planning application.

Data Description

The analysis uses two primary data sources from FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data):

Series Description Frequency Period
CES0500000003 Average Hourly Earnings (Private) Monthly 1964–2024
CPIAUCSL Consumer Price Index (All Urban) Monthly 1964–2024

Real wages computed as: Real Wage = (Nominal Wage / CPI) × 100, with base period set to allow direct dollar interpretation.

Methodology

The analysis employs a multi-model approach with rigorous validation:

01

Exploratory Analysis & Stationarity Testing

Decomposition into trend, seasonal, and residual components. ADF and KPSS tests for unit root detection. Identification of structural breaks using Chow test.

02

SARIMA Modeling

Grid search over (p,d,q)×(P,D,Q,s) parameter space. Model selection via AIC/BIC with diagnostic checking (Ljung-Box, residual normality, heteroscedasticity tests).

03

LSTM Deep Learning

Sequence-to-sequence architecture with attention mechanism. Hyperparameter tuning via Bayesian optimization. Dropout regularization to prevent overfitting.

04

Ensemble & Uncertainty Quantification

Weighted ensemble combining SARIMA and LSTM predictions. Bootstrap confidence intervals (1000 replications) for robust uncertainty estimates.

Results & Findings

15% Improvement vs SARIMA Ensemble outperforms single models
94% CI Coverage Bootstrap intervals well-calibrated

Key Findings

  • Ensemble superiority: The weighted SARIMA-LSTM ensemble consistently outperformed individual models across all forecast horizons.
  • Structural breaks matter: Models accounting for the 2020 COVID shock significantly outperformed those assuming continuity.
  • Uncertainty calibration: Bootstrap confidence intervals achieved near-nominal coverage (94% for 95% CI), essential for policy use.
  • LSTM captures nonlinearity: Deep learning component particularly valuable during high-volatility periods.

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions

  • CPI accurately captures inflation relevant to wage earners
  • Historical patterns provide information about future dynamics
  • Structural relationships remain approximately stable

Constraints

  • Aggregate data masks heterogeneity across industries/demographics
  • Monthly frequency limits analysis of high-frequency dynamics
  • Cannot incorporate exogenous policy shocks prospectively

Limitations

  • Aggregate analysis: Results represent average effects; individual/sectoral experiences may differ substantially.
  • Exogenous shock sensitivity: Models cannot anticipate unprecedented events (pandemics, policy regime changes).
  • CPI limitations: Official inflation measures may not capture experienced inflation for all demographic groups.
  • Forecast horizon: Accuracy degrades significantly beyond 12-month horizon due to compounding uncertainty.
