Computational Biology & Biomarker Research

MDD Biomarker Discovery

Blood transcriptomic analysis for Major Depressive Disorder using cell-type deconvolution and consensus clustering across multiple GEO datasets.

GitHub Repository Paper (Coming Soon)

Problem Statement

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) affects over 280 million people worldwide, yet diagnosis relies entirely on subjective clinical assessments. There are no validated blood-based biomarkers for MDD, making objective diagnosis, treatment selection, and treatment response monitoring extremely challenging.

This project aims to identify robust blood transcriptomic signatures that can differentiate MDD patients from healthy controls, with a focus on understanding the underlying cell-type specific changes.

Why This Problem Matters

Current MDD diagnosis is entirely symptom-based, leading to:

  • Delayed diagnosis (average 6-8 years from symptom onset)
  • High misdiagnosis rates (up to 50% in primary care)
  • Trial-and-error treatment selection
  • No objective measures for treatment response

Blood-based biomarkers could transform MDD care by enabling objective diagnosis, personalized treatment selection, and quantitative monitoring of treatment response.

Data Description

This study integrates three publicly available datasets from the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO):

Dataset Tissue MDD Samples Control Samples Platform
GSE98793 Whole Blood 128 64 Affymetrix
GSE76826 PBMCs 32 32 Illumina
GSE39653 Whole Blood 26 41 Affymetrix

Total: 186 MDD patients and 137 healthy controls. All datasets underwent standardized preprocessing including background correction, quantile normalization, and batch effect correction using ComBat.

Methodology

The analysis pipeline consists of four major phases:

01

Data Preprocessing & Integration

Quality control, normalization, batch effect correction, and cross-platform harmonization of gene expression data.

02

Differential Expression Analysis

Identification of differentially expressed genes using limma with empirical Bayes moderation, controlling for confounders.

03

Cell-Type Deconvolution

Estimation of cell-type proportions using CIBERSORTx and xCell to identify cell-type specific changes in MDD.

04

Consensus Clustering & Validation

Identification of MDD subtypes using consensus clustering, followed by cross-dataset validation and pathway enrichment.

Key Findings

  • Endothelial dysfunction signature: Consistent reduction in endothelial cell-associated gene expression across all three datasets, suggesting vascular involvement in MDD pathophysiology.
  • Molecular subtypes: Consensus clustering revealed three distinct MDD subtypes with different immune profiles and treatment response patterns.
  • Validation: Core signature of 47 genes validated across all three independent datasets with AUC > 0.75.

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions

  • Blood transcriptome reflects, at least partially, CNS pathology in MDD
  • Deconvolution algorithms trained on reference profiles are applicable to MDD blood
  • Batch effect correction adequately addresses technical variation between datasets

Constraints

  • Reliance on publicly available datasets with limited clinical metadata
  • Cross-sectional design prevents causal inference
  • Computational deconvolution vs. experimental single-cell validation

Limitations

  • Sample heterogeneity: MDD is clinically heterogeneous; our datasets lack detailed phenotyping for subtype analysis.
  • Medication effects: Most patients were on antidepressants, potentially confounding gene expression signatures.
  • Cross-sectional design: Cannot distinguish state vs. trait markers or establish causality.
  • Computational deconvolution: Cell-type estimates are computational inferences, not direct measurements.
  • External validation: Requires prospective validation in independent clinical cohorts.
